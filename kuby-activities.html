Kuby Chapter 1, Activity 1, Questions 1.1 to 1.9: "Map Scale Computations"

Questions 1.1 to 1.3 assume a scale of 1:25,000. Note that this scale is different from those in either of the examples just given.

1.1. Suppose that City A is 4 centimeters away from City B on the map. How many centimeters apart are they on the surface of the Earth?

They are 100, 000 centimeters apart on the surface of the Earth.

City A and City B are 100,000 centimeters apart on the surface of the Earth (25,000 centimeters times 4 = 100,000 centimeters).



1.2. How many kilometers apart are Cities A and B on the surface of the Earth?

Since there are 100, 000 centimeters in a kilometer, Cities A and B are 1 kilometer apart on the surface of the Earth.

Cities A and B are 4 kilometers apart on the surface of the Earth (since 25,000 centimeters is equal to one kilometer. That means that 4 centimeters on the map makes 100,000 centimeters on the Earth, and 100,000 centimeters equals to 4 kilometers.)



1.3. A bakery needs to supply bread to every store within a 6-kilometer radius. How many centimeters will this radius be when drawn on the map? Hint: First convert 6 km to cm, and then multiply by the representative fraction: 1/25,000.

The bakery would need to supply bread to every store within a 24 cm radius on the map.

1 kilometer = 100,000 centimeters 6 kilometers = 600,000 centimeters 600,000 centimeters divided by 25,000 = 24 centimeters The radius will be 24 centimeters when drawn on the map.
